Inside Bets: The Path to Higher Payouts

Inside bets are placed on the inner, numbered section of the betting mat. These bets cover fewer numbers, making them riskier, but they offer significantly higher payouts in return.

  • Straight Up: A bet on a single, specific number. This is the most volatile bet but offers the highest payout of 35:1.

  • Split Bet: A bet on two numbers that are adjacent on the layout (horizontally or vertically). You place the chip on the line that separates them. This pays 17:1.

  • Street Bet: A bet on a single horizontal row of three numbers. You place the chip on the outer edge of that row. A winning Street bet pays 11:1.

  • Corner Bet: A bet on a block of four numbers that form a square on the layout. You place the chip on the corner where the four numbers intersect. This pays 8:1.

  • Six Line Bet: A bet covering two adjacent Street bets (six numbers in total). The chip is placed on the outer corner shared by the two rows. This pays 5:1.

Outside Bets: Your Foundation for Steady Play

Outside bets are placed on the boxes surrounding the main number grid. They cover large groups of numbers, offering a much higher probability of winning but with smaller, even-money or 2:1 payouts. These are the ideal bets for beginners.

  • Red or Black: The most iconic bet in roulette. Youâ€™re simply wagering on the colour of the winning number. This pays 1:1.

  • Odd or Even: A bet on whether the winning number will be odd or even. This also pays 1:1.

  • High or Low (1-18 or 19-36): A bet that the winning number will be in the lower or upper half of the numbers. This pays 1:1.

  • Dozens: A bet on one of three groups of 12 numbers (1st 12, 2nd 12, 3rd 12). This pays 2:1.

  • Columns: A bet on one of the three long vertical columns on the layout, each containing 12 numbers. This also pays 2:1.
